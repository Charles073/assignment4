Group Processes

As a group in assignment 2 we worked very well. We thoroughly communicated through discord, teams, classes and weekly meetings (recorded) to discuss and understand what needs to be done as well as help eachother out if we are unsure. We were mostly passionate with what we were working on so it was great to share our input and listen to everyone. However we did have trouble getting our parts done on time and some members weren’t able to progress with their parts until the others were done. In this assignment (3), we will still have the same modes of communication through different platforms and meetings. However for the team we will be introducing smaller and doable deadlines for paragraphs, concepts and model building. This will allow us to work on our time management and give suitable time for everyone to finish their parts that are needed for others.

For the more technical processes we will be working in a similar document layout for this assignment and completing each text part. Correspondingly the website will be updated with a new template but have the same process of adding in the text as we progress with writing (However mostly having commits towards the end). As this is a more developmental assignment the new introduced processes will include a designing, a modelling, a developing and a testing procedure, unlike in the previous project.

In the designing process we will formulate a rough concept of a classroom and develop it into a more refined direction. After the design we can model or gather objects for the classroom. In the developing process the design and gathered/modeled objects will be incorporated into a built VR class world. The testing procedure will be an active process of testing each stage of the project to debug, make sure controls work and the VR world is running correctly.

Group comparison

Some of the common trends within everyone's career plans is obviously the use of IT within everyone's desired industries. Everyone needs practical skills such as understanding a variety of programming languages whilst only half need more designing-oriented skills. Most of the team also share an interest in doing independent work through freelancing and setting up their own companies. To go down this path, most of the team wants to work with other teams/companies to grow networking connections and assets before pursuing a more independent route.

Differentiations within everyone's roles mainly includes the even distribution of our group wanting to go into different careers such as web UI/UX design (Beverly & Kaylyn), software developers (Dante, Charles, Vansh & Alisa), game developers (Dante & Alisa) and mostly juxtaposing, because of Eric’s biology major, a student lab assistant. A major difference is how everyone's career approach will be such as contract work (Dante), freelancing (Beverly, Kaylyn & Alisa), business owners (Vansh, Charles & Alisa) and mostly company experience with most members.

Kaylyn’s and Beverly’s career plan is similar as they are wanting to pursue UX and UI, correspondingly in web development. Dante’s and Alisa’s direction are also alike as they want to pursue software development and then ideally go into game development. Vansh and Charles also want to pursue software development and other similar roles, however as they progress with more experience they plan to create their own company. Eric’s plan is more different to the groups as he wants to seek working as a lab assistant after gaining company experience from a biotech business rather than IT like everyone else. There are equally similarities in specific groups of peoples plans as well as an outlier smaller group with a different modal to others. Collectively everyone prefers to be building towards working independently or in a small team, however Vansh’s and Charles’ plan is to build a company involving more people. Over time everyone in the group needs similar skills to achieve their plans.
