Group Reflection

A personal assessment of how each team member has developed during this project

Eric Lin (s3844221)

The group did overall very well in the communication and labour division department. Everyone understood what was expected of them and completed them despite interruptions and circumstances. While I believe we did a good job as a whole, I feel like I did not contribute as well as others on the team. Other subjects greatly split my attention and I was not able to complete my tasks at a satisfactory level. What surprised me though was the understanding on the part of my group. Adjusting around not only mine but also the circumstances of other members. Allowing us to adjust our tasks to be manageable and thus still contribute to the group.

Beverly Li Fa Mu (s3784880)

Like always, communication between group members is our biggest strength, as a matter of fact, it was better than the last assignment. There were some members who were not able to complete specific tasks or struggled with certain aspects, such as using Blender, but as a result of our communication and transparency, we were able to make arrangements to compromise with their situations, such as hosting help-sessions and rearranging work-loads.

I strongly believe that one thing that could be improved would be to have more group members involved in the practical part of the project. Although creating in Blender has a large learning curve and requires a lot of persistence to use, I believe that we could have worked on even larger scale objects as opposed to simply creating organs. I also would have liked some members to be more involved with the game development and Unity process.

There isn't much more that I learnt about groups that I haven't already learnt from my last assignment, although I can say that I have experienced the importance of transparency between team members and the positive impacts it can bring. Although due to my shyness and tendency to overload myself with work, it becomes difficult to express my exhaustion without feeling an overwhelming amount of shame and embarrassment. Despite this, i hope to overcome those feelings; and to put it simply- cut myself some slack.

Group Reflection (Eric Lin)

Overall, it was clear that our group had fantastic chemistry, able to communicate both clearly and efficiently. We seemed to just click in a way and become quick acquaintances and friends. Everyone clearly understood their roles and tasks and were able to complete them to a great degree of satisfaction. While many of us suffered interruptions, delays and other detrimental circumstances, surprisingly however, the group as a whole adapted and was flexible enough in our labour distribution that we were able to complete the same expected amount of work with everyone still pulling their own weight. Despite that, it has to be said that some group members exceeded what was needed of them, either due to passion for the project or simple determination to do a good job. As such, a few group members expressed regret for not being able to pull their weight as much as others did, however the expression of regret is the first sign of improvement. Everyone learnt and understood that their group members were willing to help each other to overcome difficulty, and as such everyone became comfortable with overcoming their own difficulty. Many times a group member would join a meeting even if they were on the train or bus, even if they couldn’t talk they would contribute via text messages. Despite that however, more people could have joined in on the practical work.

Whilst almost everyone learned Blender despite having no prior experience, some people put more work into the creation of the project than others. Alisa, Beverly, Charles and Vansh did the majority of the work for the actual creation of the project, everyone else contributed an organ from Blender and mostly worked on the documental aspects of the project. While we efficiently distributed jobs and completed tasks, it left some of us having not touched all aspects of the project, which was detrimental as a learning experience. Such a problem has no easy fix, we were arguably only able to finish the project as efficiently because everyone understood their roles, but that left some of us having contributed less overall. If we had more time to do the project again, we could have everyone train new skills to contribute with.

Overall, the group did very well to complete everything, and our group chemistry informs us that we would be better in the future as well.
